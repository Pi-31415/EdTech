Exciting and unique classes offered by very passionate OxBridge / Ivy League graduates for middle and high school students

STEM

Game Development

Data Analysis with Excel

Excel is a powerful data analysis tool that is widely used in real life. Teaching students Excel is an effective way to help them analyze and visualize data sets. Excel skills will further prepare them for more complex problems in areas like Math, Science, and Business.

Young Engineer

Hands-on examining disassembled gadgets is the best way to foster students’ curiosity towards their surrounding. Under guidance, students will understand and appreciate the design and wisdom behind essential home appliances like clocks, speakers and radios.

Humanities / Social Sciences

Young Socrates

What’s a better way to develop critical thinking skills by delving into the philosophical and political debates in classical and modern writings, including freedom and fate, mind and body, right and wrong, power and justice? Join us and be a Young Socrates!

Psychology

This course will highlight some interesting experiments within the field of psychology. We will explore the brain and the cognitive abilities like memory, learning, attention, perception and consciousness.

Global Crises

Pressing global crises like global warming, plastic waste or human trafficking are often mentioned in schools but just in token manners. Students will get to study each of them in a data driven approach to put the scale and urgency of these crises in perspective.

Business / Finance

Stock Investing

This course is a step-by-step bottom-up approach teaching students the basics of long-term fundamental investing. Students will work in a group to prepare a stock recommendation as the final project.

How Markets Work

Ever wondered what the financial news is all about? This course will give an overview of global financial markets and macroeconomics. Upon completion students will gain the ability to understand financial news on media like Bloomberg.

Business Case Analysis

By examining business cases this course will introduce basic concepts of business management, strategy, operations and ethics. The final group project will be a presentation analyzing a business case.

Other Specialties

Law

A basic overview of different areas in law, including criminal law, contract law, and tort. Mooting (simulated court proceeding) will be conducted to let students develop skills that are necessary for a successful law student.

Architecture

Introduction to the different aspects of architecture such as form, space, order, and hierarchy. Throughout the course students will have the opportunities to produce creative works, which can be included in the portfolio for university application in the future.

Electronic Music Composition

Technology has changed music - performing is not just about mastering an instrument, and composing has never been easier. In this course students will be exposed to the frameworks and tools that allow them to express themselves creatively through sounds and experiments.
